Need Immediate Help?

If your life is at risk right now

You are not alone. We are here to Help.

If you feel like you might attempt suicide, or may have seriously harmed yourself, you need urgent medical help. Please:
  • Call 999 for an ambulance
  • Go straight to A&E, if you can
  • Call your local crisis team, if you have their number
  • If you can't do this by yourself, ask someone to help you.
Mental health emergencies are serious. You're not wasting anyone's time.

Managing Stress and Anxiety


Relaxation

Hand Trace Breathing

Trace up your finger while breathing in, down while breathing out. Repeat across all five fingers for a calming rhythm.


Grounding

5-4-3-2-1 Technique

Look around and name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, and 1 you taste.


Thinking Skill

Fact or Fear?

When a worry appears, ask: “Is this based on facts, or is it fear talking?” Helps bring thoughts into balance.


Self-Care

Two-Minute Reset

Write: “Right now I feel… What I can control today is… One small thing that could help me is…”


Movement

Shake It Out

Shake your hands, arms, and legs for 10 seconds to release built-up tension.


Creative

Free Doodle

Doodle anything for 2–3 minutes. No rules, no pressure — just let your hand move.

Building Healthy Habits

  • Start your day with a positive affirmation.
  • Check in with your feelings at least once a day.
  • Write or voice-note your thoughts.
  • Notice at least one good thing that happens each day.

  • Limit screens 30 minutes before bed.
  • Go to bed and wake at consistent times.
  • Set a wind-down routine (reading, music, meditation, stretching).
  • Let your body rest when you feel tired.

  • Drink plenty of water,
  • eat regular snacks and meals,
  • move your body for a few minutes every few hours,
  • get outside for fresh air and natural light.

  • Talk to someone regularly (friend, family member, teacher).
  • Spend time with people you trust,
  • reach out when you feel lonely or overwhelmed,
  • show kindness to yourself and others.

  • Take mini screen breaks.
  • Put your phone on Do Not Disturb before bed or when overwhelmed.
  • Notice when scrolling makes you feel worse and take a break.
  • Follow accounts that inspire and uplift you.
