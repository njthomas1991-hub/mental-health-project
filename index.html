Managing Anxiety

Relaxation

Hand Trace Breathing

Trace up your finger while breathing in, down while breathing out. Repeat across all five fingers for a calming rhythm.

Grounding

5-4-3-2-1 Technique

Look around and name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, and 1 you taste.

Thinking Skill

Fact or Fear?

When a worry appears, ask: “Is this based on facts, or is it fear talking?” Helps bring thoughts into balance.

Self-Care

Two-Minute Reset

Write: “Right now I feel… What I can control today is… One small thing that could help me is…”

Movement

Shake It Out

Shake your hands, arms, and legs for 10 seconds to release built-up tension.

Creative

Free Doodle

Doodle anything for 2–3 minutes. No rules, no pressure — just let your hand move.

Healthy Habits

  • Start your day with a positive affirmation to set a hopeful tone.
  • Check in with your feelings at least once a day.
  • Write or voice-note your thoughts.
  • Notice at least one good thing that happens each day.

  • Limit screens 30 minutes before bed.
  • Go to bed and wake up at consistent times.
  • Set a wind-down routine: reading, music, meditation, or stretching.
  • Let your body rest when you feel tired.

  • Drink plenty of water throughout the day.
  • Eat regular snacks and meals.
  • Move your body for at least 5 minutes every few hours.
  • Get outside for fresh air and natural light daily.

  • Talk to someone regularly: friend, family member, or teacher.
  • Spend time with people you trust who feel safe.
  • Reach out when you feel lonely, overwhelmed, or need support.
  • Show kindness to yourself and others.

  • Take mini screen breaks during the day.
  • Put your phone on Do Not Disturb before bed.
  • Notice when scrolling makes you feel worse and take a break.
  • Follow accounts that inspire and uplift you.
